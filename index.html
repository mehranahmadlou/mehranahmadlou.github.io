about me
Mehran Ahmadlou

I'm fascinated by how our brains shape who we are and what we do. As a Group Leader at Oxford, I dive deep into the neural circuits that drive our behaviors and decisionsâlike what makes us stick with a goal or suddenly switch direction.

In my lab, we blend cutting-edge techniques to watch brain circuits in action. Using tools like optogenetics and real-time imaging, we're mapping the neural highways that control both everyday behaviors and those affected in conditions like ADHD, autism, and depression. Some of my most exciting discoveries have revealed how the brain's deeper structures fuel curiosity, handle stress, and help us make choices.

Before Oxford, I worked at UCL's Sainsbury Wellcome Centre, unraveling how the brain drives exploration. My PhD research at Amsterdam's Vrije Universiteit earned me the 'Dutch Neuroscience Thesis Prize' for my work on visual processing.

While the lab keeps me busy, I'm equally passionate about mentoring the next generation of scientists and sharing neuroscience with the public. When I'm not chasing neural circuits, you'll find me on the badminton court, tinkering with electronics, solving math problems, or learning something completely new.
